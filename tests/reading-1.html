Passage: The History of Chocolate

Chocolate has a history that spans over 3,000 years. The earliest evidence of chocolate consumption dates back to the ancient Maya and Aztec civilizations in Mesoamerica. These cultures prepared chocolate as a bitter drink, often mixed with spices or corn puree.

When Spanish explorers arrived in the Americas in the 16th century, they brought chocolate back to Europe. Initially consumed primarily by royalty and the upper classes, chocolate gradually became more widely available with the advent of the Industrial Revolution. The invention of the cocoa press in 1828 by Coenraad van Houten revolutionized chocolate production, allowing for the creation of cocoa powder and solid chocolate.

Today, chocolate is enjoyed worldwide in various forms, from dark and milk chocolate to white chocolate. The global chocolate industry is worth over $100 billion annually, with Switzerland, Belgium, and Germany leading in per capita consumption.

Key developments in chocolate history:

  • 1500 BCE: First evidence of cocoa consumption by Olmecs
  • 600 CE: Maya establish cocoa plantations
  • 1502: Columbus encounters cocoa beans
  • 1828: Cocoa press invented
  • 1847: First solid chocolate bar created
  • 1875: Milk chocolate invented
